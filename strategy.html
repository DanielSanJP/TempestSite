We provide universities and research centres with data for studies and curriculum enhancements facilitating deep explorations and encouraging innovation in future space exploration.     Scope

    Scope Included

    The scope of our project consists of the comprehensive design and fabrication of the satellite, ensuring it is capable of housing all our necessary equipment and withstanding the harsh conditions of launching and orbiting space. Our technical team will integrate a range of advanced equipment, including visual spectrum cameras, infrared cameras, ultraviolet cameras, magnetometers, metal detectors, gravimeters, light intensity sensors, and radio spectrum detection devices. Additionally, we will implement a propulsion system that utilizes interactions between orthogonal electric fields, along with a Radio Thermal Generator (RTG) and solar panel arrays for power generation, and a battery bank for energy storage.

    The project encompasses the development and execution of extensive testing protocols to validate the performance and reliability of both hardware and software components under simulated space conditions. Our software team will develop and run computational models and simulations to support technical and systems analysis. The team assigned to this project includes a Technical Manager with five staff members, a Software Manager with five staff members, an Engineering Manager with nine staff members, and a Telemetry Manager with two staff members, all contributing their specialized skills to ensure the success of the mission.

    Scope Excluded

    This project does not include the development or manufacturing of the launch vehicle(rocket), which will be provided by Rocket Lab. Rocket Lab will manage the launch operations, including the logistics and integration of the satellite into the rocket, with our team providing support as needed. Additionally, the creation or maintenance of satellite communications systems, such as ground-based satellite dishes and the necessary fibre optic network for data transmission, will be handled by Rocket Lab and its network.

    Legal authority to operate space vehicles and secure necessary governmental approvals will be managed by the New Zealand Space Agency and Rocket Lab, with our involvement limited to the application process. The transportation of the satellite to the launch site will be conducted by a reliable transportation company, separate from our operations.
